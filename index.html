Paint of the Week

Marco Ortolan is an Argentine artist (draftsman, painter) who started out as an impressionist painter (the school in which he began painting), moved through hyperrealism, and today, generally in the language of realism, is highly personal. Finished with plastic that gives a unique impression. It does not preclude entry into other themes, such as ``the artist (as a painter) must be universal'' (which supports Leonardo da Vinci's idea in his theory of painting). Today, his techniques are loosely expressed, with plasticity and impressionistic tendencies, which emerge through the process of each composition, such as scraping with a spatula or scratching to incorporate the tones of the support. The process begins with loose drawings assembled in a grid to solidify the proportions, then the first tones emerge and from there the plasticity begins to dominate the scene back and forth until it is adapted to the same artist's demands.
